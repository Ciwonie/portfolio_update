About Me


I’m Jonathan, a full stack web developer and student. This fall, I’ll begin my senior year of Physics and Mathematics at Loyola University Chicago. I have spent the past three years working in conjunction with my academic pursuits, including a study abroad at Oxford University. As a Loyola Freshman, I was selected to be both a tutor in mathematics and a teaching assistant in physics. I was also accepted into two research positions within the Physics Department—one with a publication pending.

Prior to college, I served honorably in the United States Air Force from 2010 – 2014. Stationed in colorful Colorado, I was assigned to the 2nd Space Systems Operations Squadron as a Satellite Systems Operator. At the age of 18, I began my training to launch, maneuver, and repair GPS satellites. My affinity for all things space, science, and technology rocketed from here.

If I’m not developing websites or studying for an exam, I enjoy travelling with a book in hand. Reading and exploring are two of my favorite hobbies. I’ve been from coast to coast in the United States, have travelled to two Canadian provinces, and crossed the Atlantic—exploring Iceland, Netherlands, Spain, and the U.K. If I’m not fortunate enough to be travelling, guarantee I’ll be lost in a book.
